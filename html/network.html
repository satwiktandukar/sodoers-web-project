NETWORKING




Introduction

A network is simply a group of two or more Personal Computers linked together. Many types of networks exist, but the most common types of networks are Local-Area Networks (LANs), and Wide-Area Networks (WANs). In a LAN, computers are connected together within a "local" area (for example, an office or home). In a WAN, computers are further apart and are connected via telephone/communication lines, radio waves or other means of connection




Why we need Networking?

Networking isn't only great for when you're looking for a new job. Career networking should become a part of your daily schedule. Your career network should be in place for when you need it, both for job seekers and for moving along the career ladder. This article will teach you everything you need to know. Networking can be a good way to hear about job opportunities or get an "in" at the company you'd like to work in.
